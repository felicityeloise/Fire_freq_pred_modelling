Description

Abstract / Description:
History of planned burns and wildfires on and around the Protected Areas and Forests of Queensland.
Purpose:
Contains the extent of the burn scars for fires on the QPWS protected areas and forests as well as the ignition date and the date the fire was declared out. While this dataset can be used to determine the frequency of fires at a location, the dataset is not complete as historical records were entered in an ad hoc fashion from paper-based records and the recollection of QPWS rangers.
Additional Information:
It should be noted that many of the burn scars have been captured from field observations and digitisation in a mapping application from reference datasets (e.g. topographic, cadastral data) and as such will not exactly match scars shown in imagery. Most historical records were captured this way.
Because different fires will have different intensity and not all vegetation will be burnt in a fire, a percentage of the area that burned has been estimated for some of the records.
Not all records were captured with the date that the fire was extinguished. For these records the year and month the fire was declared out are calculated by adding seven days to the ignition date. Some fires burn over several months and as a result the out year and month should be treated as indicative only.
For fires that are currently burning, or have not been declared out, the shape of the burn scar will in general only be indicative. More effort is exerted to accurately capture the final area of the fire after is has been extinguished.
Due to the way the planned burns are managed, some planned burns are conducted over multiple stages, to achieve an outcome that would not be possible if the burn was conducted in one stage. The records in this dataset only represent the final overall burnt area with no indication of which areas were burnt at certain stages. Such fires will generally have an out date that is several months after the ignition date.

Data Quality

Data quality scope:
Level: dataset
Completeness (omission):

This dataset contains historical records captured from the recollection of QPWS rangers and officers prior to the implementation of the fire planning and management system. As such the data is incomplete and does not represent all fires that have occurred on protected areas and forests.
Consistency (conceptual):

Attribute information is consistent with the source data as captured by QPWS rangers and officers.
Positional accuracy (external, absolute):

Positional accuracy is dependent on the method of capture.
Attribute accuracy (non quantitative):

Where the areas for the extent of the fire are calculated the accuracy of the area is dependent on the positional accuracy of the spatial data.

History:

Lineage statement:
This dataset is derived from databases maintained by QPWS rangers and officers at park bases. Rangers use various techniques to capture the extent of fires including capturing on paper plans on site, digitising from aerial imagery, vegetation layers and other spatial information using Geographic Information System (GIS) software, capturing with devices that use the Global Position System (GPS) or capturing from fire scar analysis of satellite imagery. While for most records the information about the fire is captured at the time of the fire, records of historical fires have been placed into the dataset.
